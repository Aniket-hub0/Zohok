Prototype

Prototype 1

involves engaging hemiplegic patients in a VR cricket game using the Oculus Quest 2. A single IMU sensor captures the motion of the disabled arm, with initially scaled movements. As performance improves, the difficulty increases. Patient data is stored locally and accessible via USB. This prototype has been developed and a preliminary clinical investigation done.

Prototype 2

Prototype 2 is currently being developed and shares similarities with the first, having a multi-level VR game console that offers a variety of games for different physiotherapist-recommended movements. It employs multiple IMU sensors, capturing joint angles from various body locations. Data is stored in the cloud, accessible to authorized personnel, and includes a dedicated dashboard for physicians to monitor patient progress.
